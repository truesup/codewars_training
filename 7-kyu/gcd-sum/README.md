<h2><a href=https://www.codewars.com/kata/5dd259444228280032b1ed2a/train/javascript target="_blank">GCD sum</a></h2><h3>7 kyu</h3><p>Given the sum and gcd of two numbers, return those two numbers in ascending order. If the numbers do not exist, return <code>-1</code>, (or <code>NULL</code> in C, <code>tuple (-1,-1)</code> in C#, <code>pair (-1,-1)</code> in C++,<code>None</code> in Rust, <code>array {-1,-1} </code> in Java and Golang).</p><pre><code>For example: Given sum = 12 and gcd = 4...solve(12,4) = [4,8]. The two numbers 4 and 8 sum to 12 and have a gcd of 4.solve(12,5) = -1. No two numbers exist that sum to 12 and have gcd of 5.solve(10,2) = [2,8]. Note that [4,6] is also a possibility but we pick the one with the lower first element: 2 &lt; 4, so we take [2,8].</code></pre><p>More examples in test cases. </p><p>Good luck!</p>
